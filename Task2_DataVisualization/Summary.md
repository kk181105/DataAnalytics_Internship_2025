**Task 2 Summary – Netflix Data Visualization & Storytelling**

This summary highlights the insights discovered by visually analyzing Netflix’s content catalog using the cleaned Netflix dataset.

________________________________

 1. Content Type Distribution
**Chart:** Donut  
**Insight:**  
- Movies make up the majority of Netflix’s catalog.  
- TV Shows represent a smaller proportion but are steadily increasing.  

Netflix has a movie-heavy content strategy, encouraging quick consumption and higher daily engagement.

___________________________________

 2. Top 10 Content Producing Countries
**Chart:** Horizontal Bar  
**Insight:**  
- USA dominates by a large margin.  
- Secondary contributors: UK, Spain, Germany, India.  

This shows Netflix’s strong reliance on US content while slowly expanding into global markets.

___________________________________

 3. Content Addition Over Time
**Chart:** Line with Area Fill  
**Insight:**  
- Sharp growth from 2016–2020.  
- Peak additions in 2019–2020.  
- Slight stabilization afterwards.  

Reflects Netflix’s aggressive global expansion strategy and pandemic-induced demand spike.

___________________________________

 4. Rating Distribution
**Chart:** Pie  
**Insight:**  
- TV-MA (Mature) is the most common rating.  
- Large share of PG-13 and TV-PG indicates significant family content.  

Netflix targets an adult demographic while still maintaining family-friendly options.

____________________________________

 5. Release Year Analysis
**Chart:** Histogram  
**Insight:**  
- Majority of content released between 2017–2020.  
- Very little pre-2000 content.  

Netflix focuses on fresh, modern programming with high engagement potential.

____________________________________

 6. Top Genres/Categories
**Chart:** Treemap / Bar  
**Insight:**  
Top categories include:  
- International Movies  
- Dramas  
- Comedies  
- Action & Adventure  
- Documentaries  

These genres reflect universal viewer preferences and Netflix’s global push.

___________________________________

 7. Movie Duration Distribution
**Chart:** Box Plot  
**Insight:**  
- Most movies range from 85–110 minutes.  
- Median duration ~99 minutes.  

Consistent with standard movie lengths and optimized for viewer attention spans.

___________________________________

 8. Content Addition by Month
**Chart:** Heatmap  
**Insight:**  
- High additions in January, July, and December.  
- Low additions in February and May.  

Netflix aligns releases with holidays, vacations, and subscription cycles.

___________________________________

 9. TV Show Seasons Analysis
**Chart:** Column Chart  
**Insight:**  
- 65% of shows have only 1 season.  
- Only a small percentage extends beyond 3 seasons.  

Netflix focuses on limited series, reducing long-term production costs.

___________________________________

 10. Directors with Most Content
**Chart:** Lollipop  
**Insight:**  
- No extreme dominance — diverse set of directors.  
- Raúl Campos and Jan Suter lead with 18 titles each.  

Netflix collaborates widely, supporting an international creator ecosystem.

__________________________________-

 Final Takeaway
Netflix’s content strategy emphasizes:
- Modern, recently released content  
- Movie-heavy catalog  
- Mature audience targeting  
- Strong global expansion  
- Genre diversity focused on drama, suspense, and international works  

These insights create a comprehensive understanding of Netflix’s evolving platform strategy.
